Residency Services

Certified IT professionals embedded within your team — on-site or remote — to accelerate projects, fill critical skill gaps, and transfer knowledge directly to your staff.

What Are Residency Services?

Residency services place Snowlake's certified engineers directly within your organization for a defined period. Unlike traditional consulting where experts come and go, our residents become part of your team — attending your standups, working in your systems, and solving your daily challenges alongside your staff.

This model is ideal when you need specialized expertise for a project, want to upskill your internal team, or need to maintain critical systems during a transition or peak period without committing to permanent headcount.

Engagement Models

  • Project-Based Residency — An expert embedded for the duration of a specific project (e.g., cloud migration, OpenShift deployment).
  • Staff Augmentation — Extend your existing team capacity with senior engineers on a flexible schedule.
  • Knowledge Transfer Residency — Expert works alongside your team to upskill them on new technologies over an agreed period.
  • Interim Management — Experienced IT leadership to cover gaps during transitions or reorganizations.

Expert Profiles Available

  • Red Hat Certified Engineers (RHCE / RHCA)
  • VMware Certified Professionals (VCP / VCAP)
  • AWS Certified Solutions Architects
  • Kubernetes & OpenShift specialists
  • Network and security engineers
  • Data center infrastructure specialists
  • IT project managers and architects

Why Choose Residency Over Traditional Consulting?

Traditional consulting engagements can feel disconnected — the expert arrives, delivers, and leaves before your team fully understands what was built. Residency is different:

  • Deep integration with your team and processes
  • Continuous availability throughout the engagement
  • Knowledge stays in your organization after the engagement ends
  • Faster ramp-up — no repeated context-setting meetings
  • Flexible duration — weeks, months, or ongoing
